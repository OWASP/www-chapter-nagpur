## Call for Speakers

We are always looking for speakers to present at OWASP Nagpur chapter meetups.

Whether you are a security researcher, developer, penetration tester, student, or enthusiast — if you have knowledge to share, we want to hear from you. All talks must be vendor-neutral and aligned with OWASP's open and non-commercial values.

### Topics of Interest

- OWASP Top 10 (Web, API, Mobile)
- Web and Mobile Application Security
- Cloud Security and DevSecOps
- Penetration Testing and Ethical Hacking
- Threat Modeling and Secure Design
- Capture The Flag walkthroughs
- Privacy, Compliance and GDPR
- AI and ML Security
- Independent security research

### How to Submit

Send your speaker proposal to **[aishwary.gathe@owasp.org](mailto:aishwary.gathe@owasp.org)** with the subject line: `[Speaker Proposal] Your Talk Title`

Please include the following in your email:

**Speaker Information**
- Full Name
- Job Role / Title
- Company or Organization
- City and Country
- Email and Contact Number
- LinkedIn or GitHub profile (optional)

**Talk Details**
- Title of the Presentation
- Abstract / Summary (200 to 400 words)
- Estimated Duration (20 / 30 / 45 minutes)
- Talk Level (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)
- Any prerequisites for the audience

### What We Offer

- A welcoming audience of security professionals and enthusiasts
- Promotion on our [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/owaspngp/) page
- Networking opportunity with the Nagpur security community
- Recording and slides shared with the community (with your permission)

### Speaker Agreement

By presenting at an OWASP Nagpur Chapter event, speakers agree to the [OWASP Speaker Agreement](https://owasp.org/www-policy/operational/speaker-agreement). All presentations must be vendor-neutral.

---

Lightning talks (5 to 10 minutes), demos, and beginner-level sessions are very welcome. We value diverse perspectives from all backgrounds.
